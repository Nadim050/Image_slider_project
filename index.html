FLOWER
A flower is the specialized reproductive structure of angiosperms, featuring four main whorls—sepals, petals, stamens, and pistils—attached to a receptacle. They are designed to attract pollinators via color and fragrance, producing seeds through sexual reproduction. Essential parts include stamens (male) and pistils (female).

NATURE
Nature is the physical world and all living things—plants, animals, ecosystems, and natural landscapes—that exist independently of human creation. It acts as a complex, self-regulating system encompassing geological, atmospheric, and biological processes. Often viewed as a source of beauty and life, it is crucial for survival, providing essential resources like air, water, and sunlight.

PLANT
Plants are multicellular, eukaryotic organisms belonging to the kingdom Plantae that produce their own food via photosynthesis using chlorophyll. They are characterized by rigid cell walls made of cellulose, a sedentary lifestyle, and a life cycle involving alternation of generations. Plants are essential for life, providing oxygen and food for ecosystems.
